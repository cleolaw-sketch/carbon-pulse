India emits a fraction of what wealthy nations do per person, yet bears a disproportionate share of climate consequences. A carbon market isn't just an economic instrument here. It is a negotiating position and a signal to the world.

The Legal Architecture

Three layers. One market.

India's carbon market is built on the Energy Conservation Act — amended in 2022 to explicitly authorise carbon trading. On top of that sits the Carbon Credit Trading Scheme, which defines who participates and how credits are issued. The rules and methodologies underneath specify exactly how entities measure, report, and trade.

How the market works

The Carbon Credit Trading Scheme (CCTS) creates compliance obligations for 800+ industrial units across 9 hard-to-abate sectors, launched under the Energy Conservation Act 2022. Here's the five-step cycle every obligated entity goes through.

01
Step 1 of 5
Targets set
BEE assigns emissions intensity targets to 800+ industrial units across 9 hard-to-abate sectors — steel, cement, aluminium, fertilisers, refineries, textiles, chlor-alkali, paper & pulp, and petrochemicals. Targets are based on sector-specific benchmarks derived from historical performance data.
02
Step 2 of 5
Measure & report
Obligated entities track their greenhouse gas output across the compliance cycle and submit detailed reports. Accredited Carbon Verification Agencies (ACVAs) independently audit the data before it is accepted by BEE.
03
Step 3 of 5
Credits issued
Entities that outperform their intensity targets receive Carbon Credit Certificates (CCCs) — each representing the reduction, removal, or avoidance of 1 tonne CO₂e. Issued by BEE upon Central Government approval, credited to accounts maintained by GRID-INDIA's registry.
04
Step 4 of 5
Trade on exchange
CCCs are traded on power exchanges on a mandatory monthly basis. CERC approves a price floor and forbearance ceiling; the market discovers the actual price within that band. Entities cannot sell more than their registry holdings — GRID-INDIA cross-checks all bids.
05
Step 5 of 5
Compliance & enforcement
Entities that missed their targets must purchase enough CCCs to cover the shortfall before the compliance deadline. Three defaults in a single quarter results in a six-month trading ban, without prejudice to further penalties under the Energy Conservation Act.

The evolution of India's carbon market

Key policy milestones

Now
CCTS Pilot Phase — Registry Live
The pilot phase of CCTS is underway with approximately 300 large industrial facilities obligated. Grid-India manages the national carbon credit registry. The first compliance cycle is active with sector-specific baselines being established.
2023
CCTS Notified
The Carbon Credit Trading Scheme is officially notified by MoEF. It establishes a compliance market (mandatory for Designated Consumers) and an offset market (voluntary). BEE is designated the administrator.
2022
EC Act Amended — Carbon Markets Legalised
The Energy Conservation (Amendment) Act 2022 explicitly enables the establishment of a carbon market in India. This legislative change gave MoEF the authority to notify a Carbon Credit Trading Scheme.
2012
PAT Scheme Launched
Bureau of Energy Efficiency launches the Perform Achieve & Trade scheme — India's first domestic market-based energy efficiency mechanism. Designated Consumers in 8 industrial sectors receive specific energy consumption targets.
2005
CDM Participation Begins
India joins the Clean Development Mechanism under the Kyoto Protocol, becoming one of the largest CDM credit suppliers globally. Projects in renewables, energy efficiency, and waste management generate Certified Emission Reductions (CERs).
